Cold prep tastes better than room temperature. Refrigerate your prep solution. Some people freeze it into slush. Use a chilled glass.

Drinking through a straw placed toward the back of your mouth helps bypass taste buds. Some people even use a straw and pinch their nose.

Have something to chase immediately after each drink: ginger ale, clear hard candy, lemon wedge to suck on. Just make sure it's clear and not red/purple.

Drink a glass every 10-15 minutes. Setting a timer helps you stay on track without dwelling on it.

üì± Stay Distracted

Watch a movie, read, or have someone keep you company. The time passes faster when you're not focused on the prep.

üß¥ Protect Your Skin

All that bathroom time can irritate skin. Use soft toilet paper or flushable wipes (or baby wipes). Apply barrier cream (Vaseline, Desitin, A&D ointment) proactively.

Clear Liquids You Can Have

‚úÖ Allowed

  • Water, sparkling water
  • Clear broth (chicken, beef, vegetable)
  • Coffee or tea (no milk or cream)
  • Clear juices (apple, white grape)
  • Sports drinks (Gatorade, Powerade‚Äînot red/purple)
  • Jell-O (not red or purple)
  • Popsicles (not red or purple)
  • Clear hard candy
  • Honey

  • Anything red, purple, or blue (can look like blood)

  • Juice with pulp

  • Any solid food

Prep Day Shopping List

  • Prescription prep solution
  • Clear broths (chicken, vegetable)
  • Jell-O (yellow, green, orange)
  • Sports drinks (yellow or clear)
  • White grape juice or apple juice
  • Ginger ale or Sprite
  • Hard candy (butterscotch, lemon drops)
  • Soft toilet paper or flushable wipes
  • Barrier cream (Vaseline, Desitin)
  • Entertainment (books, movies, phone charger)

The Day Before: What to Expect

  • Morning: You can usually eat a light breakfast (check your instructions), then clear liquids only
  • Afternoon/Evening: Start drinking prep. Effects begin 1-3 hours later
  • Evening: Frequent bathroom trips. Stay close to a bathroom. Wear comfortable, easy-off clothing
  • Late night: Trips taper off. You can usually sleep (set alarms if you have morning prep doses)

Split-Dose Prep (Recommended)

Many doctors now recommend split dosing: half the prep the evening before, half the morning of the procedure. Research shows this is more effective and easier to tolerate.

  • You'll wake early (typically 4-5am) to finish
  • Allow 4-5 hours between finishing prep and procedure time
  • Yes, the early wake-up is worth it‚Äîbetter prep = better exam

How Do You Know the Prep Worked?

Your output should be clear or light yellow, like urine. If it's still brown or has particles, your colon may not be clean enough, which can mean repeat procedure or missed findings.

If you're concerned your prep isn't working, contact your doctor's office.
