After your colonoscopy, you'll get results about what was found. Here's what the different findings mean and when you'll need your next screening.

Possible Findings

âœ… Normal / No Polyps

Great news! Your colon looked healthy with no abnormal growths. This is the most common result.

Follow-up: Next colonoscopy in 10 years (average risk patients)

Hyperplastic Polyps

Small, usually harmless growths. These are NOT precancerous. They're very common, especially in the lower colon (rectum and sigmoid).

Follow-up: Usually 10 years (same as normal), unless large or numerous

Adenomatous Polyps (Adenomas)

These ARE precancerousâ€”they can become cancer over time (usually 10-15 years). Removing them prevents cancer. This is why colonoscopy is so valuable.

Types:

  • Tubular adenomas: Most common type, lowest risk
  • Tubulovillous adenomas: Intermediate risk
  • Villous adenomas: Higher risk, especially if large
Follow-up: 3-5 years depending on number, size, and type

Sessile Serrated Polyps (SSPs)

Flat polyps that can be harder to see. Also precancerous, especially when large or showing dysplasia.

Follow-up: 3-5 years depending on findings

Advanced Adenoma

An adenoma that's either large (â‰¥10mm), has villous features, or shows high-grade dysplasia. Higher risk = closer follow-up.

Follow-up: 3 years

Colorectal Cancer

If cancer is found, you'll be referred to specialists (oncology, surgery) for staging and treatment planning. Caught early, colorectal cancer is highly treatable.

Follow-up: Treatment plan with oncology team

Waiting for Biopsy Results

If polyps were removed, they're sent to pathology. Results typically take 1-2 weeks. Your doctor will contact you with findings and follow-up recommendations.

It's normal to feel anxious waiting. Remember: most polyps are not cancer, and even precancerous polyps are a success storyâ€”you found and removed them before they became dangerous.
