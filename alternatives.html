Screening Alternatives

Updated Feb 2026 · 10 min read

Colonoscopy isn't the only way to screen for colon cancer. Here are the alternatives, their pros and cons, and when they make sense.

The Options

FIT (Fecal Immunochemical Test)

Every 1 year

A simple at-home stool test that detects hidden blood in your stool. You collect a small sample and mail it to a lab.

✅ Pros

  • Easy—done at home
  • No prep, no sedation
  • Inexpensive
  • No time off work

❌ Cons

  • Must do yearly
  • Only detects bleeding lesions
  • Positive = need colonoscopy anyway
  • Misses some polyps

Cologuard (Stool DNA Test)

Every 3 years

An at-home test that checks stool for both blood AND abnormal DNA from cancer/precancer cells. More sensitive than FIT but more expensive.

✅ Pros

  • Done at home
  • No prep or sedation
  • Detects more than FIT alone
  • Every 3 years (less frequent than FIT)

❌ Cons

  • Higher false positive rate
  • Positive = need colonoscopy
  • More expensive
  • Still misses some polyps

CT Colonography (Virtual Colonoscopy)

Every 5 years

A CT scan that creates 3D images of your colon. Requires bowel prep (like regular colonoscopy) but no sedation.

✅ Pros

  • No sedation needed
  • Shorter procedure
  • Sees entire colon well
  • May find issues outside colon

❌ Cons

  • Still requires full bowel prep
  • Radiation exposure
  • Can't remove polyps—need colonoscopy if found
  • May find "incidentalomas"

Flexible Sigmoidoscopy

Every 5 years (or 10 with FIT)

Like a colonoscopy but only examines the lower third of the colon. Less prep, shorter procedure, usually no sedation.

✅ Pros

  • Minimal prep
  • Usually no sedation
  • Can remove polyps found
  • Quick procedure

❌ Cons

  • Only sees 1/3 of colon
  • Misses right-sided cancers
  • Less commonly offered now

When Is Colonoscopy Still Best?

  • Higher risk: Family history, personal history of polyps, inflammatory bowel disease
  • Symptoms: Blood in stool, change in bowel habits, abdominal pain
  • Positive stool test: FIT or Cologuard positive means colonoscopy is needed
  • Preference: One-and-done for 10 years (if normal)

Important: Any Screening > No Screening

The best screening test is the one you'll actually do. If you're avoiding colonoscopy and doing nothing, an alternative test is FAR better than no test.

The Bottom Line

Colonoscopy is the most thorough option and the only one that can prevent cancer (by removing polyps). But stool tests are reasonable alternatives for average-risk people who won't do colonoscopy.

Talk to your doctor about which option makes sense for your risk level and preferences.
